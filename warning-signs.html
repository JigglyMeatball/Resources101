Safety first

Warning signs after an accident

Use this page as a safety reminder. It does not diagnose injuries. When symptoms are severe, worsening, unusual, or concerning, seek professional care.

Seek emergency help now for symptoms like these

  • Trouble breathing or chest pain
  • Heavy bleeding
  • Loss of consciousness, fainting, confusion, or seizure
  • Severe or worsening headache
  • Repeated vomiting
  • New weakness, numbness, or trouble walking
  • Severe neck, back, abdominal, pelvic, or chest pain after trauma
  • Vision changes, extreme drowsiness, or inability to wake normally
  • A child acting unusually after an injury

What if I am not sure?

If you are unsure whether a symptom is serious, call a qualified healthcare professional, urgent care, nurse line, poison control when relevant, or emergency services. Do not use this website to decide that a serious symptom is safe.
